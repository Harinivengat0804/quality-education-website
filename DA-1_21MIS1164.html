Quality Education (SDG 4)

Ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education and promoting lifelong learning opportunities for all.

Sustainable Development Goal 4 (SDG 4) focuses on ensuring inclusive, equitable, and quality education for all while promoting lifelong learning opportunities. Education is a fundamental human right and a critical driver of sustainable development. However, millions of children and adults worldwide still lack access to quality education due to poverty, conflict, discrimination, and inadequate infrastructure.

SDG 4 aims to address these challenges by improving educational accessibility, increasing the number of qualified teachers, and providing safe and inclusive learning environments. It also emphasizes the need for equal access to education regardless of gender, socioeconomic status, or disability. By fostering knowledge and skills through formal and informal education, SDG 4 helps create informed citizens who can contribute to economic growth, social development, and environmental sustainability.

One of the key targets of SDG 4 is to ensure free and equitable primary and secondary education for all children by 2030. Additionally, it encourages lifelong learning by expanding access to vocational training and higher education. Achieving this goal requires global cooperation, investment in education systems, and policy reforms that support inclusive learning opportunities. Quality education empowers individuals, breaks the cycle of poverty, and lays the foundation for a more just and prosperous world.

10 Targets of SDG 4

  1. Free primary and secondary education
  2. Equal access to quality pre-primary education
  3. Equal access to affordable technical, vocational, and higher education
  4. Increase the number of people with relevant skills for financial success
  5. Eliminate all discrimination in education
  6. Universal literacy and numeracy
  7. Education for sustainable development and global citizenship
  8. Build and upgrade inclusive and safe schools
  9. Expand higher education scholarships for developing countries
  10. Increase the supply of qualified teachers

Problem Statement

Addressing Challenges in Achieving SDG 4

Despite significant progress in global education, millions of children, youth, and adults still lack access to quality education. Barriers such as poverty, gender inequality, conflict, disability, and inadequate infrastructure prevent equitable learning opportunities, making Sustainable Development Goal 4 (SDG 4) a crucial global priority.

One of the major challenges is the lack of access to free and inclusive education. In low-income countries, children are often forced to drop out due to financial constraints, lack of schools, or poor learning conditions. Gender disparities remain a pressing issue, with girls in many regions facing cultural and societal restrictions that limit their educational opportunities. Additionally, children with disabilities often lack the necessary resources and specialized support to receive quality education.

Another significant problem is the shortage of trained teachers and insufficient learning materials. Many developing countries struggle with underfunded education systems, overcrowded classrooms, and outdated curricula that fail to equip students with relevant skills. Furthermore, digital learning gaps, especially in rural areas, have widened disparities in education accessibility.

Without urgent action, the lack of quality education will continue to hinder social and economic development. Addressing these issues requires global cooperation, increased investment in education, and policies that ensure inclusive and equitable learning opportunities for all.

Book Donation Drive - Trichy

To promote the joy of reading and foster knowledge, the Aruppe Library of St. Joseph’s College (Autonomous), Trichy, organized a book donation drive today, February 14, 2025.

The primary aim of this noble initiative is to encourage intellectual growth!

In a special event held at the Trichy Central Prison, more than two hundred valuable books were formally handed over to the Superintendent of the prison. This book donation drive has been successfully conducted for two consecutive years.

The Superintendent of Trichy Central Prison, along with the teachers serving there, expressed heartfelt gratitude for this generous contribution. They also conveyed their appreciation, noting that this initiative exemplifies the college’s continued commitment to education, social responsibility, and community welfare.

Art in Prison Initiative

It is not about reformation or therapy but recognising that a prison is a smaller part of the society we live in, at least that is the philosophy behind ‘Siraigalil Kalai’. They believe in slipping in art, not just as a creative outlet for inmates but as a way to deconstruct the limitations for everyone else too.

The heart of this ‘Art in Prison’ initative is about bringing folk art, theatre, botanical cyanotype art, and music into Puzhal-1 Central Jail in Chennai, continuing for a year now. The initiative is led by Sumanasa Foundation and Project 39A, and Chennai photo Biennale’s invited photography exhibition was showcased at Madras Literary society on Saturday.

And one of the new additions to the initiative is a book drive — organised in collaboration with Nam Veedu Nam Oor Nam Kadhai — that has already received about 500 books, both second-hand and brand new, including short stories, non-fiction, and even works by Rhonda Byrne, all within the first day. It is running until February 28 for anyone to donate in person at Madras Literary Society inside DPI complex or contact Nam Veedu Nam Oor Nam Kadhai on social media for postal address, after which the books will be sent to the inmates of Puzhal jail.
